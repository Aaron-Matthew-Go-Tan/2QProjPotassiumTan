Notable Species: Bee




Have you ever wondered how your fruits and vegetables even make it to your plate? Part of this is due to the efforts of bees. Read on to learn more about them!

According to the Editors of Encyclopaedia (1999), bees are any of more than 20,000 species in the suborder Apocrita. They are very small animals, with the adults clocking in about 2 mm to 4 cm in size. In addition, aside from the social hive-dwellers we’re all familiar with, there exist solitary bees that burrow in the ground and provide for their young solo.

'
Most bees, unlike the closely related wasps (which are carnivores), feed their young with a combination of pollen and nectar. They obtain these resources by gathering them from flowers; however, in doing so, some of the gathered pollen gets on the flowers they visit next, pollinating them.

This act of “accidental” pollination helps in the production of many common food items such as apples, tomatoes, various beans, watermelons, broccoli, and much more. This hugely beneficial prospect is a valuable asset to our food supply, but it is in danger of being stripped from us.



Pesticides, parasites, and worst of all, climate change, among a myriad of other factors, have caused bee populations to plummet in recent years. So, what can we do to save these bees and our meals? Check this page for more info!
